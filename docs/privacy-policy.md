# Privacy Policy for Hiker

_Last updated: REPLACE_WITH_DATE_

Hiker ("the app", "we", "us") is an offline-first hiking app. This policy explains
what data the app handles, where it is stored, and the limited cases in which data
leaves your device. **Hiker has no user accounts and no backend server of its own.**
All of your content stays on your device unless you explicitly choose to share it.

## Summary

- We do **not** collect, sell, or transmit your personal data to us — we have no server.
- All your hiking data is stored **locally on your device**.
- We use **no analytics or crash-reporting** services.
- We show ads through **Google AdMob**, which may collect device and advertising
  identifiers to serve ads. You control personalized ads via the consent prompts
  described below.
- The app contacts a few third-party map and weather services to function; these
  receive only map tile or coordinate requests, never your identity.

## Data stored on your device

The following are saved in a private on-device database and never automatically
leave your device:

- **Location / GPS tracks** — latitude, longitude, altitude, speed, accuracy, and
  timestamps recorded while you are actively recording a hike.
- **Routes and waypoints** — including any you import or create.
- **Journal entries** — your notes and references to photos you attach (photos stay
  in your device storage; we store only their file paths).
- **Emergency contacts** — names and phone numbers you add for the safety features.
- **App preferences** — theme, language, and map layer settings.

This data is stored unencrypted within the app's private storage. Your device's
own operating-system encryption (when enabled) protects it at rest. If you enable
your platform's device backup (iCloud / Google), this data may be included in those
encrypted backups under your platform provider's terms.

## Location use

Hiker requests location access to show your position on the map and to record your
hike. With your permission, recording can continue in the **background** (while the
screen is off) so a hike is captured even when the app is not in the foreground. On
iOS a status indicator is shown while background location is active; on Android a
persistent notification indicates recording is in progress. Recording only happens
while you have explicitly started it, and you can stop it at any time. Recorded
location data is written only to the on-device database.

## When data leaves your device

Data leaves your device only in these cases:

1. **Map tiles (MapTiler)** — to display maps, the app requests map tiles from
   MapTiler. These requests contain map tile coordinates only, not your identity.
   Downloaded tiles may be cached on your device for offline use. See MapTiler's
   privacy policy: https://www.maptiler.com/privacy-policy/
2. **Weather and elevation (Open-Meteo)** — optionally, the app sends a set of
   coordinates to Open-Meteo to retrieve weather at the start of a hike and
   elevation data when planning a route. No personal identifier is sent. See
   https://open-meteo.com/en/terms
3. **Route search (Overpass / OpenStreetMap)** — when you search for nearby routes,
   the app sends your search coordinates or a search keyword to a public Overpass
   API endpoint. No personal identifier is sent.
4. **Sharing you initiate** — when you export a GPX file, share your location, or
   send an SOS message, your device's native share / messaging / dialer is used and
   you choose the recipient. The app never sends these automatically and never
   transmits them to us.

5. **Advertising (Google AdMob)** — the app displays ads served by Google AdMob.
   To request and measure ads, Google may collect device information and identifiers
   (including the advertising identifier), approximate location derived from your IP
   address, and ad-interaction data. See the "Advertising" section below and Google's
   policy: https://policies.google.com/technologies/ads

These third parties operate under their own privacy policies. The app functions
offline; if these services are unreachable, the app degrades gracefully — ads simply
do not load and the rest of the app is unaffected.

## Advertising

Hiker shows ads through **Google AdMob** (banner, interstitial, and rewarded ads).
AdMob is provided by Google and may collect and process device and advertising
identifiers, coarse location inferred from your IP address, and ad-engagement data
to deliver and measure ads.

- **Consent (GDPR / EU).** On first launch, users in regions that require it are
  shown a consent form (via Google's User Messaging Platform) to choose whether ads
  may be personalized.
- **App Tracking Transparency (iOS).** On iOS, the system asks for your permission
  before the advertising identifier is used for tracking. If you decline, you will
  still see ads, but they will not be personalized.
- Ads only load when the device is online; while hiking offline, no ad data is
  requested.

For details on how Google uses this data, see
https://policies.google.com/technologies/partner-sites

## Safety / SOS features

The SOS and emergency features let you dial emergency numbers, compose an SMS to
your saved contacts, and share your current coordinates. These actions are always
initiated by you and use your device's native phone, messaging, and share
functions. Hiker is **not** an emergency dispatch service and does not transmit
your location to any emergency service automatically.

## Children's privacy

Hiker is not directed at children and does not knowingly collect personal data
from children.

## Your control over your data

- You can delete individual hikes, routes, journal entries, and emergency contacts
  within the app.
- You can use the **Clear all data** option in Settings to erase all of your hiking
  content from the device at once.
- Uninstalling the app removes all on-device data.
